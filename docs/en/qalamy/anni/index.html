Are you not astonished?!

Dear brothers. Of all the things said during his presidency, the two passages that troubled me most are written below.

1. The 19th radio address by President Mohamed Nasheed, delivered via the Voice of the Maldives

… In my view, the sensible course is that the taking of a person’s life should not be a punishment laid down in the Penal Code. Killing people should not be something prescribed and carried out by law in the Penal Code. I therefore hope that the Majlis will pass this bill in line with this thinking.

Similarly, the cutting off of hands has not been a common practice in our country up to now. On both of the occasions when it was carried out, the harms and ill consequences that emerged from it were just as wide and just as great. In my view, I do not see this as something that should be written into the Penal Code of the Maldives and carried out in that way …

2. Words delivered by President Mohamed Nasheed at the Land Allocation ceremony

… I am in no doubt as to what the people of Malé desire, nor what the people of the entire Maldives desire. They do not wish for hands to be cut off in this country, nor for executions, nor for stoning — and they do not wish, you understand, …

Are these not irreligious statements?!

And yet — that there are those who hold that the ones to be arrested and stripped of authority for being called irreligious are the religious scholars themselves: that truly astonishes me.
