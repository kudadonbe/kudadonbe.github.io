The Pillars of Iman

The six matters of faith that every Muslim believes in.

Muhammad bin Jamil Zino · The Pillars of Islam and Iman (أركان الإسلام والإيمان)

To believe in:

  1. Alláh (in His Existence, His Oneness in His Attributes, His deserving to be worshipped and supplicated and His right to legislate).

  2. His angels (beings created from light for obeying Alláh's Orders).

  3. His Books (the Torah, the Gospel of Jesus, the Psalms of David and the Qur'án which is the best of them).

  4. His Messengers (the first of them was Noah and the last of them was Muhammad ﷺ, being the last of the Prophets).

  5. The Last Day [the day of resurrection for the reckoning of the people according to their deeds, and their appropriate compensation (award or punishment)].

  6. And to believe in Qadar (Divine Preordainment) (that all which occurs of good and evil is by Divine decree, accompanied by the employment of means to achieve desired results with satisfaction of what happens of good or bad, the sweet and the bitter, because it occurs by Alláh's Decree).
